If you are a salaried employee and are a Malaysian or a Permanent Resident in Malaysia, every month, when paying out your salary, your employer is required to withhold the portion of your pay that needs to go into your EPF accounts. That portion will be paid into your EPF accounts as your employee’s contribution.

What is EPF

The Employees Provident Fund (EPF) in Malaysia is a compulsory savings scheme where both employees and employers contribute a fixed percentage of the employee's monthly salary, currently set at 11% of earnings up to a maximum of RM6,000 per month.

This fund serves as a financial safety net for employees during retirement and also supports various life milestones such as home ownership and medical expenses.

It plays a crucial role in securing financial stability for Malaysian workers throughout their careers and into retirement.

Income tax rates depend on an individual's tax residency status. You will be treated as a tax resident for a particular Year of Assessment (YA) if you are a:

  1. Malaysia Citizen or Permanent Resident who resides in Malaysia except for temporary absences; or
  2. Foreigner who has stayed / worked in Malaysia (excludes director of a company) for 183 days or more in the year preceding the YA.

Otherwise, you will be treated as a non-resident of Malaysia for tax purposes.

Malaysia's personal income tax rates for resident taxpayers are progressive. This means higher income earners pay a proportionately higher tax, with the current highest personal income tax rate at 30%.
